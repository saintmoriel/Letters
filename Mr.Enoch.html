Dear Mr. Enoch Daniel Ojonimi,

I want to start by saying something that I think you already know about yourself, but that I want to say anyway — you are one of the smartest people I have ever been in a room with. Not in a way that is comfortable or casual. In a way that makes you sit up straighter. In a way that makes you not want to waste the hour.

That is who you are to me.

My first impression of you never really left. Mathematician. Computer scientist. Someone who carries standard not as a performance but as a way of being. You introduced me to cloud computing and something clicked that has not unclicked since. That subject, that world — I fell in love with it in your class. That is yours. You did that.

But here is the fuller picture.

I was afraid of you. Not of you as a person, but of not being enough in your presence. The same way I sometimes felt around the sharpest people in any room, I felt it around you. And there were moments, I will be honest, where that fear made me pull back. Where I underperformed not because I could not, but because some quiet part of me had already decided the gap was too wide.

But I want you to know something else. Unlike some of those moments in my journey where fear made me disappear completely — with you, I stayed. I was afraid and I still came. I could approach you, talk to you, sit with the discomfort and not run from it. You were my project supervisor, and I showed up for that. That took something from me. And you made it possible by being who you are — intimidating, yes, but never closed.

What you did not do with words, you did with your presence. Every time I was around you, something in me wanted to be more structured. More serious. More intentional. You never had to say "do better" — you just existed, and that was enough.

You kept me on my toes. You still do, even now as I walk out of these gates.

I carry your standard with me. Into everything I am building, into every room I will walk into from here. And I hope that someday the work I do will reflect that — that someone will look at what I have built and see the fingerprints of people like you in it.

Thank you, sir. For the fear that made me grow. For the standard that refused to leave me alone. For being exactly the kind of person worth being inspired by.

With deep respect and gratitude,
