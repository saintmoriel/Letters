Before you press play

Dear Dr. Felix,

I am not sure I have the right words for this. But I have never really needed the right words around you — and I think that is the whole point.

From the moment I met you, I knew you were different. Not just as a lecturer, but as a person. Brilliant, yes — genuinely one of the sharpest minds I have sat under. But what set you apart was never just the intelligence. It was the warmth. The openness. The way you made a room feel safe enough to think out loud in.

You saw me.

I do not say that lightly. There are lecturers who teach you, and then there are people who see you……who look past the grades and the nervousness and the rough edges and call out something in you that you had not yet fully named yourself. You were the second kind. You saw the leader before I fully believed I was one. You put me in front of people, trusted me to carry teams, trusted me to speak, trusted me to represent — and every single time, I wanted to be exactly who you believed I was.

When I became NACOS president, you were there before the title even existed. Speaking to students on my behalf, standing behind me quietly, making sure I was not walking into it alone. I do not know if you know how much that meant. I do not know if you know that I carried that into every decision I made in that role.

Even TEDx — watching you spearhead that, watching you pull people together and make something real out of nothing — that was a masterclass. Not just in event management. In what it looks like when someone leads from genuine investment in the people around them.

Here is what I want you to know: every time I underperformed, I felt it. Not because of fear, but because of you. Because I knew what you had put in me, and I did not want to waste it. That guilt — the good kind — kept me reaching further than I would have on my own.

You are one of the reasons I leave Veritas knowing who I am.

And this is not really a goodbye. It is more of a see you soon. Because I intend to build something……something real, something that matters — and when I do, I want to come back and stand in front of you and say: this is what your investment looked like when it grew. I would be proud to work alongside you someday. Prouder still to call you a mentor beyond these walls.

Thank you, sir. For seeing me. For staying. For being exactly who you are.

With love and gratitude,

When you are ready — press play.

"I am grateful for the privilege of the path."

With love and gratitude, Yusuf Nimrod Mainasara
